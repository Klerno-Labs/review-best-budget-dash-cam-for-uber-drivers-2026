Buyer's Guide

Video Quality


When choosing a dash cam, prioritize video quality. Higher resolutions (like 4K) provide clearer and more usable footage, which can be crucial in accidents.

Night Vision


For Uber drivers who often drive at night, good night vision is essential. Look for models with features like HDR or low-light sensors to ensure clear footage in dim conditions.

Parking Mode


Parking mode is a valuable feature for protecting your vehicle when parked. This mode records any incidents while your car is stationary, offering peace of mind for rideshare drivers.

Storage Options


Consider the storage options provided by the dash cam. Some models offer local storage via SD cards, while others provide cloud storage, which can help manage footage effectively.

FAQ

What features should I look for in a dash cam?


Look for high resolution, good night vision, reliable parking mode, and ease of installation. Additional features like GPS tracking and Wi-Fi connectivity can also enhance usability.

How much should I spend on a budget dash cam?


A good budget dash cam typically ranges from $70 to $150. Investing in a model within this range can yield reliable performance without breaking the bank.

Is cloud storage necessary for dash cams?


Cloud storage can be beneficial for accessing footage remotely, but it's not strictly necessary. Many drivers are satisfied with local storage, especially if they regularly manage their footage.

What is the best resolution for a dash cam?


The best resolution for a dash cam is typically 1080p or higher. 4K resolution is ideal for capturing fine details, but 1080p is sufficient for most drivers.

Can I use a dash cam for ridesharing?


Absolutely! Dash cams are highly recommended for rideshare drivers as they provide evidence in case of disputes or accidents, protecting both the driver and the passengers.
